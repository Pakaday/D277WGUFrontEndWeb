Ohio at a Glance

Ohio, often called The Buckeye State, became the 17th state to join the Union on March 1, 1803. Its capital and largest city, Columbus, is a thriving hub for culture, business, and innovation. Ohio is home to over 11 million residents, many of whom proudly refer to themselves as Buckeyes.

Ohio's Rich History

Ohio has a storied past filled with notable firsts. It’s the birthplace of aviation legends Orville and Wilbur Wright, and it holds a special place in space history as the home state of Neil Armstrong, the first man to walk on the moon. Ohio also boasts the nickname “Mother of Presidents,” being the birthplace of eight U.S. Presidents, including Ulysses S. Grant and William Howard Taft.

Natural Beauty

From the serene shores of Lake Erie in the north to the rolling hills of Appalachia in the southeast, Ohio is a state with diverse natural beauty. Whether you're looking to hike scenic trails of relax by the water, Ohio offers a wide range of outdoor activities to explore.

Industry and Innovation

Ohio has long been a leader in industry, especially in manufacturing and automobiles. Today, it continues to be a center for innovation, particularly in the fields of aerospace and technology, keeping the pioneering spirit alive.

Sports and Entertainment

Ohioans are passionate about their sports! The state is home to multiple professional teams, including the Cleveland Browns (NFL), Cincinnati Reds (MLB), Cleveland Cavaliers (NBA), and Columbus Blue Jackets (NHL). The capital, Columbus, is also home to The Ohio State Buckeyes (NCAA). Whether it’s football, baseball, or hockey, Ohio’s sports culture is something to experience.

State Symbols

Ohio's state bird, the Northern Cardinal, and its state flower, the Scarlet Carnation, reflect the natural beauty and pride of the state. These symbols are celebrated throughout Ohio, representing admiration and deep connection to the state's identity.
