About Columbus

Columbus is the capital and largest city of Ohio, with a population of over 920,000 people. Founded in 1812 and incorporated in 1816, it has grown into a thriving urban hub located in the central part of the state. The city is known for its strong economy, driven by industries such as finance, education, technology, and health care. Major employers include Nationwide Insurance, Ohio State University, and Battelle Memorial Institute.

Columbus boasts a diverse population and cultural scene, with a mix of urban and suburban areas. It's home to various attractions, such as the Columbus Museum of Art, Franklin Park Conservatory, and the popular Short North Arts District. With a median household income of around $58,000, Columbus is slightly above the state average. The city offers a relatively affordable cost of living, and its steady economic growth continues to attract new residents and businesses. As a rapidly expanding metro area, Columbus blends the vibrancy of a large city with the charm of a close-knit community.
